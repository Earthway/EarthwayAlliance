Our Impact in Action and Testimonials

Restoring Wetlands, Transforming Lives Earthway Alliance is actively working with local communities to restore degraded wetlands across Baringo, Elgeyo Marakwet, Nakuru, and Uasin Gishu counties. Our mission is to improve access to clean water, boost local biodiversity, and build climate-resilient ecosystems that benefit both people and nature. Through participatory restoration efforts, we are empowering communities to reclaim and protect these vital ecosystems. From replanting native vegetation to establishing sustainable water management practices, our initiatives are rooted in collaboration and long-term impact. "Earthway Alliance, through Amos the CEO, has taught our community many practical ways of restoring wetlands. Today, we have safer and cleaner water for our village," shared one of the respected community elders. This is just one example of how knowledge-sharing and grassroots leadership are transforming lives. Mission

At Earthway Alliance, we are dedicated to preserving and restoring wetland ecosystems as a nature-based solution to combat climate change (SDG 13), protect biodiversity (SDG 15), and enhance water quality and availability for communities (SDG 6). Our approach is grounded in community engagement, innovative conservation strategies, and a shared commitment to advancing the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals for a more sustainable and resilient planet.

Vision

To be a leading organization in climate impact solutions, promoting environmental conservation, community-driven sustainability initiatives, and leveraging carbon credits to support climate-positive actions and livelihoods.
